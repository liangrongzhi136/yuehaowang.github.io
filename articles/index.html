Proof: Existence of Linearly Independent Vectors in Images of Non-scalar-multiple Linear Transformations

This is a challenging problem in an exercise of SI131 Linear Algebra for Information Science. This article elaborates my thoughts in this problem. Proposition: Let τ1,τ2:V→V be linear transformations such that one is not a scalar multiple of the other. Suppose that dim Im(τ1),dim Im(τ2)≥2. Then there exists a v∈V such that τ1(v), τ2(v) are linearly independent.
